What is it?

ArchivesSpace is the open source archives information management application for managing and providing web access to archives, manuscripts and digital objects.

— https://archivesspace.org

Origins

  • Need
    Process and manage archival holdings and their metadata; efficiency, scale, sophistication
  • Software Origins
    Grew out of previous archiving software: Archivists' Toolkit and Archon
  • Historical Processes
    Always consortial, grant-funded at first, launched 2013
  • Membership Model
    54 institutions at first, including Yale

Change over Time

  • Now in version 3.4.0
  • Nearly 500 member institutions worldwide (predominantly in USA and Global North)
  • 1000s of collections and MMs of objects to few collections and objects
  • R1s to SLACs to public schools to museums to social organizations

But what is it?

Allows us to consolidate in a single piece of software a wide range of intellectual, financial, managerial, and other professional archives work.

It is* to special collections what Voyager and ORBIS are to general collections

Administrative interface for staff as well as a public discovery interface

Not all places use the public interface. We didn't at first.

*Still getting there, perhaps, but close enough to make the assertion
