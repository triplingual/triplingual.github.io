Conference Talk

Encoding Reparative Description: Developing Tools to Analyze Problematic Finding Aids

Team: Ella Li, Jesse Johnston, Ricky Punzalan, and Deirdre De La Cruz (all UMich)

Key Takeaway: Trying to "address historical injustices, colonial legacies, and systemic biases associated with . . . collections" in part by developing ways to automate diagnosing finding aids needing remediation

https://osf.io/5w7ez

Conference Talk

The Compassion of DevOps

Speaker: Bess Sadler

Key Takeaway: "Elevate the improvement of work over the doing of work"

https://osf.io/4pqfc

Keynote 2

A Feminist Data Manifest-No

Speaker: Dr. Patricia Garcia from UMSI

Key takeaway: A critical "No" is an important response to harmful data practices; we should be looking carefully at our own to eliminate harm.

https://www.manifestno.com/

Workshop: Collection Builder

Team: Olivia Wikle, Devin Becker, Evan Williamson

Description: Jekyll+Rake–based tool to go from a spreadsheet (CSV, Google Sheet) to a web interface for a collection (any aggregation of metadata)

Key Takeaway: Another great possibility in the space of online exhibitions

https://collectionbuilder.github.io/
